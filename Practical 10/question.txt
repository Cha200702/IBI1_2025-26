Result:

Over time, the relationship between DALYs (Disability-Adjusted Life
Years) in China and the UK has shifted toward greater similarity. This
convergence is driven by a narrowing gap in absolute DALY values and
parallel trends in the underlying patterns of national health burden.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
1990: SIGNIFICANT DIVERGENCE IN HEALTH BURDEN
--------------------------------------------------------------------

In the early years of the dataset (1990), the two countries exhibited
stark differences in their DALY profiles:

- China: Approximately 2,800 DALYs per 100,000 people
  - Primary drivers: Infectious diseases, maternal and child health
    issues, and limited access to advanced healthcare in rural regions

- United Kingdom: Approximately 1,600 DALYs per 100,000 people
  - Primary drivers: Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) such as
    cardiovascular conditions
  - Status: Had already completed the epidemiological transition

The UK's lower and more stable DALY rate reflected its mature
healthcare system and successful control of infectious diseases.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
1990-2019: STEADY CONVERGENCE OF DALY RATES
--------------------------------------------------------------------

Over the three decades, the DALY gap between the two countries
narrowed consistently:

- China's DALY rate declined by approximately 40% by 2019
  - Key contributing factors:
    * Expanded public health investments (vaccination, sanitation)
    * Rapid economic growth and poverty reduction
    * Universal primary healthcare coverage expansion

- UK's DALY rate remained relatively flat
  - Minor fluctuations due to stable NCD management
  - Mature healthcare system with established disease prevention
    programs

By 2019, the DALY rates had nearly converged, with both countries
recording rates between 1,600 and 1,700 per 100,000 people.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
SHARED HEALTH PRIORITIES: THE EPIDEMIOLOGICAL TRANSITION
--------------------------------------------------------------------

The convergence in DALY rates also reflects a fundamental shift in
health priorities for both nations. Non-communicable diseases have
replaced infectious diseases as the primary drivers of health burden:

- Leading causes of DALYs in both countries:
  * Heart disease
  * Stroke
  * Chronic respiratory illnesses

While small differences persist (e.g., China has slightly higher DALYs
from certain respiratory conditions linked to historical risk factors),
the overall disease burden profiles have become remarkably similar.

--------------------------------------------------------------------
CONCLUSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
The dramatic reduction in China's DALY rate over three decades
represents one of the most significant public health achievements in
modern history. The convergence with the UK's DALY profile
demonstrates that successful public health interventions and economic
development can rapidly transform a nation's health landscape. Both
countries now face similar challenges in managing the growing burden
of non-communicable diseases.
